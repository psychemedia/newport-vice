Hampshire Advertiser - Saturday 18 June 1836

On Monday, the 13th instant, the corner stone for a new church at Ventnor, Isle of Wight, was laid by Mrs. Hambrough, of Steephill Castle. This church is to be built and endowed at the sole expense of John Hambrough. Esq. of Steephill Castle;— the ground for the site of the church was given by Charles Popham Hill, esq. The numerous persons assembled to witness the ceremony were appropriately addressed, and prayers offered up by the Rev. Augustus Hewett. It will be a neat gothic structure, with a tower and spire 100 feet in height, from designs by Mr. Robert Ebbels, architect at Trysull. Staffordshire.

Public Ledger and Daily Advertiser - Wednesday 16 August 1837

Queen Adelaide.— It would be matter of great regret if her Majesty Queen Adelaide were to lose the advantage of the mild air of the Undercliff of the Isle of Wight during the next winter by any difficulty in obtaining a suitable residence. The only house on a splendid scale in that region is Steephill Castle; but if it be impracticable to obtain it for the Royal invalid, there are other mansions of inferior pretensions, but possessing the same recommendations of shelter and retirement, which might accommodate her Majesty and the small circle of friends she would probably take with her. The highest medical authorities have declared the climate of Bonchurch, and the southern coast, equal to that of Montpelier or Madeira, and we hope that no effort will spared to procure for Queen Adelaide a participation in those benefits which are obtainable by persons of inferior rank.*— Morning Paper.*

Worcester Journal - Thursday 04 August 1842

The Queen Dowager will spend the coming winter in the Isle of Wight; Steephill Castle, the seat of Mr. John Hamborough, being absolutely engaged for the winter residence her Majesty, who is to take possession on the 1st of October.*—Hampshire Advertiser.*
